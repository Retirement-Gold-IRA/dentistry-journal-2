What should I do if I have a dental emergency?

A dental emergency is any sudden problem with your teeth and gums that requires urgent medical attention. They can be scary, inconvenient and even dangerous if untreated. But thankfully, dental emergencies are relatively uncommon. In fact, statistics show that less than 5% of all dental problems require emergency care. That said, it’s still important to know what to do if a dental emergency occurs. If you experience any of these situations, call your dentist immediately and expect them to ask you a few questions about the situation and then determine if you need dental surgery, antibiotics, or both. You should expect the same kind of response from any emergency room or urgent care center if you experience a dental emergency. This article will explain how to respond to common dental emergencies and what to do if you have to visit the dentist in the middle of the night for an urgent problem.

Toothache

If you experience a sharp, stinging pain in one or more teeth, usually caused by an infection or an untreated dental abscess, you may need antibiotics and/or oral surgery to save the tooth. Otherwise, you may risk losing the tooth if the infection spreads further up the tooth’s roots. Symptoms of an infected tooth include redness, swelling, and fever that doesn’t go away after two days. If you experience any of these symptoms, see a dentist as soon as possible.If you experience a sharp, stinging pain in one or more teeth due to a cavity or other dental problem that’s progressed too far to be treated, you may be able to relieve the pain with a temporary filling. If your dentist is unable to see you right away, you can relieve the pain with a temporary filling by placing a piece of dental wax in the cavity and covering it with a piece of gauze and nail polish. This method will keep the cavity from getting infected while you wait for treatment from your dentist.

Broken Tooth

If you experience a broken tooth, you’ll probably need a trip to the dentist and an emergency appointment. If the broken tooth is causing you pain, you should see your dentist as soon as possible. If the pain isn’t too intense, you can put a small piece of dental wax into the broken teeth and cover it with a piece of gauze and nail polish. The wax will keep the tooth from getting infected until you can get to a dentist.If the broken tooth causes you pain or you notice blood in your mouth, you should go to the emergency room or urgent care center. Your broken tooth will need to be extracted before you can have it restored with a crown.

Loose or Displaced Teeth

If you have a loose or displaced tooth, you’ll need to see your dentist as soon as possible. You’ll probably need to put the tooth back in its socket, and you may need oral surgery to do so. If your tooth is preventing you from eating or speaking normally, you should see your dentist as soon as possible. If you don’t see a dentist within a day or two, make an appointment to see a doctor as soon as possible.If your loose teeth are causing you pain, you should see your dentist as soon as possible. If the pain isn’t too severe, you may be able to put a piece of dental wax into the loose teeth and cover it with a piece of gauze and nail polish. This will keep the teeth from getting infected until you can get to a dentist.

Jaw Injury

If you experience a jaw injury, you should see your dentist as soon as possible. If the injury is severe enough to require a trip to the hospital, you’ll need to inform the emergency room staff about the accident and the possible connection to your teeth. If your bite and/or alignment is affected by the injury, you’ll probably need to undergo oral surgery to repair the damage.

Dental Worker Infection

If you get an infection from a dental workite, you should see your dentist as soon as possible. If the infection is in a sensitive area, you may be able to treat it at home with antibiotics and pain medication. If your infection is in a non-sensitive area or if it’s too severe to treat at home, you’ll need to be treated by a dentist.

Conclusion

Dental emergencies are no fun, but they can be prevented with regular check-ups and cleanings. If you experience a dental emergency, make sure to call your dentist as soon as possible and expect them to ask you a few questions and then determine if you need dental surgery, antibiotics, or both. If you have to visit the emergency room or urgent care center for an urgent dental problem, you should expect the same kind of response from any emergency room. Remember, you don’t have to be afraid of dental problems — you can prevent them by seeing a dentist twice a year, flossing regularly, and brushing your teeth with a soft-bristled toothbrush.
