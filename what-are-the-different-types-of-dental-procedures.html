What are the different types of dental procedures?

When most people think of what goes on behind the scenes at the dentist's office, they probably think of drilling and filling teeth. However, dentists can perform a variety of services and procedures that don't involve the teeth. These include things like gum cleaning; restoration of teeth by taking an impression of your teeth; root canals; restoration of broken teeth; teeth whitening; oral cancer detection; and even teeth transplantation. This list isn't exhaustive, but it does give you some idea of what goes on in a dental office every day. If you're looking for a career that’s rewarding and challenging, dental assisting is the perfect profession for you. These are just a few interesting facts about dental assisting that will get you more information about this great profession.

What is dental assisting and what do dental assistants do?

Dental assisting is a professional occupation in which a dental assistant assists the dental doctor and/or dental hygienist during dental procedures. As a dental assistant you will be assisting the dental doctor or dental hygienist in the dental office, including assisting them with the use of dental instruments and equipment, providing oral care and assisting with dental procedures. There are several different roles that dental assistants can fulfill within the dental office environment, including the following:- Clinical assistant - Clinical assistants are responsible for assisting in the cleaning and restoration of teeth. This may include assisting the dental doctor or dental technician in the restoration of teeth with fillings or crowns, and cleaning teeth with special instruments. They may also assist the dental technician in making dental appliances such as dentures and retainers.- General assistance - General assistance duties include the handling of incoming calls, and general office work such as filing, copying, and mailings. These duties vary from office to office and are not always general assistance; however, they are not specialized in any one area either.- Laboratory technician - Some dental offices have a laboratory in which teeth are restored, and a dental assistant may be responsible for the testing of teeth, sterilization of instruments, and assisting in the restoration of teeth with dental prosthetics such as crowns and veneers.- X-ray technician - In some dental offices an X-ray technician is responsible for taking X-rays of patients' teeth. This is not always the case, but in some offices it is.

What is a dental assisting school and how do you become a dental assisting?

A dental assisting school is where dental assistants go to obtain their education and training in the dental assisting profession. Schools that offer a dental assisting program usually offer a combination of classroom instruction, clinical experience, and hands-on practice. While there is no strict curriculum for becoming a dental assisting student, there are certain topics that dental assisting schools cover in their programs. These include:- Anatomy and physiology - Anatomy and physiology is the study of the structure and function of the human body, including its major systems such as the nervous, cardiovascular, and digestive systems.- Dental assisting - This is the study of the different aspects of the dental assisting profession, such as proper ways to handle instruments, how to sterilize them, and the different uses of each piece of equipment.- Human growth and development - The human growth and development course covers topics such as child development and teen development, including teenage issues like teen pregnancy.

Why is the dental assisting profession so popular?

One of the main reasons for the popularity of the dental assisting profession is the fact that it is one of the few professions that can be done as either an independent contractor or an employee. This makes it an attractive profession for those who are self-employed, as well as for those who are working to support themselves while they're attending school.Another factor that contributes to the popularity of the dental assisting profession is the fact that there are numerous opportunities for advancement in the field. As a dental assistant, you will have the chance to move up the ladder and become more involved in the day-to-day operations of the dental office.

Dental assisting salary and job outlook

The average starting salary for dental assistants is between $16,000 and $30,000 per year, with the average salary increasing as experience is gained. There is also the potential for dental assisting to be a part-time job, and many dental assistants work a second job or attend school to earn extra income.There is currently a shortage of dental assistants, which means that job opportunities will likely be plentiful in the years to come. If you're interested in a career in the dental assisting field, there are numerous educational programs that can get you out there and ready for the work.
