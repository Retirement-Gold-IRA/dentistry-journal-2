What is dentistry?

Dentistry is the branch of medicine concerned with the study, diagnosis, prevention and treatment of diseases and conditions of the teeth, jaws, gums, salivary glands and related structures.The primary goal of dental professionals is to promote optimal health of the teeth and oral tissues, maintain function and aesthetics, and prevent oral diseases. The American Dental Association notes that “the practice of dentistry encompasses prevention and treatment of diseases, maintenance of healthy teeth and gums and restoration of oral function and esthetics of patients of all ages.”

What are the basics of dentistry?

Most people know that teeth are made of enamel, a hard, protective layer that covers the teeth. Behind the enamel is the dentin, a tough and elastic material that makes up most of the teeth.The gum, or gums, is the tissue that surrounds the teeth and extends below the gumline. The gums are important for a number of reasons. First, they provide a barrier between the teeth and your mouth. Second, they trap food particles and other substances, helping to prevent bacteria from entering your bloodstream.The teeth are connected to the gums by a series of ligaments and membranes. These structures are called soft tissue and are much more flexible than the teeth or gums. They are important for speech, nutrition and appearance.
